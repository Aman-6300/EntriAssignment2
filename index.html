Top Destinations

Alleppey

Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is a picturesque town in Kerala, India, renowned for its serene backwaters, houseboats, and intricate network of canals. Often referred to as the "Venice of the East," it offers visitors a tranquil escape amidst lush greenery and charming waterways.

Munnar

Munnar, a picturesque hill station in Kerala, India, is renowned for its sprawling tea plantations, misty hills, and serene landscapes. This charming destination offers a tranquil retreat with its lush greenery, wildlife sanctuaries, and breathtaking viewpoints.

Vagamon

Vagamon, a serene hill station in Kerala, India, is known for its lush greenery, rolling meadows, and cool climate. It offers a perfect getaway for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts with activities like trekking, paragliding, and sightseeing.

Athirapally

Athirapally, located in the Thrissur district of Kerala, India, is renowned for its breathtaking waterfall, often referred to as the "Niagara of India." The lush greenery, diverse wildlife, and serene ambiance make it a popular destination for nature lovers and tourists.

About Kerala

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a state in southwestern India famed for its lush landscapes, serene backwaters, and vibrant culture. Bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west and sharing boundaries with Karnataka and Tamil Nadu, Kerala's diverse geography includes beaches, hill stations, and extensive backwater networks. Its major cities include Thiruvananthapuram (the capital), Kochi, and Kozhikode. Culturally rich and historically significant, Kerala has been influenced by various civilizations through centuries of trade, resulting in a unique blend of traditions. The state is renowned for its classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, traditional martial arts like Kalaripayattu, and colorful temple festivals. With the highest literacy rate in India, Kerala also boasts impressive human development indicators and progressive social policies. Kerala's cuisine, marked by the abundant use of coconut and spices, is as diverse as its landscapes, featuring dishes like appam, puttu, and a variety of seafood. The state's natural beauty, from the backwaters of Alleppey to the hill stations of Munnar and wildlife sanctuaries like Periyar, makes it a major tourist destination, attracting visitors worldwide for its scenic charm and cultural richness.
